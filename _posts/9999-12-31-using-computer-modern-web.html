Often loved by those with an appreciation for TeX, other times reviled by those who think it's a flawed product of inertia: I'm in the former camp, but it was difficult to get a conversion that rendered better than terribly on web pages.

The sans-serif family is easy to get right, but the serif is a whole world of trouble, perhaps because of a lack of adequate hinting for low-density screens. Combined with being a didone, its hairline horizontals and serifs cause trouble outside of print.

Knuth's original Computer Modern was not available in any embeddable web font format (OpenType, TrueType, WOFF, Embedded OpenType or SVG) so we'll have to use a conversion. A quick overview of the conversion hierarchy:

  • Computer Modern — the original METAFONT source version by Donald E. Knuth
    • Computer Modern Unicode — OpenType by Andrey Panov and Nikola Lečić
      • cm-web-fonts — TrueType, WOFF, SVG and EOT by an anonymous maintainer
    • Latin Modern — OpenType by Bogusław Jackowski and Janusz Marian Nowacki
    • BaKoMa fonts — OpenType and TrueType by Basil K. Malyshev
