A selection of blogs and other websites that I find interesting enough to follow avidly.

Fabrice Bellard's Home Page

To be described as a genius would be a grossly deficient appraisement of Fabrice Bellard, the creator of FFmpeg and QEMU. Being everything a hacker aspires to, he has won the IOCCC twice, and in his spare time has written a PC emulator in JavaScript, a DVB-T transmitter by abusing a VGA controller and much more.

The Old New Thing

A Windows veteran of over two decades, Raymond Chen gives the masses an insight into just how far Microsoft has to bend over backwards for naughty third party developers depending on bugs and undocumented APIs, as backwards compatibility is carefully maintained.

Dave's Archives

The blog of David Cooper, a software engineering lecturer at Curtin University, two of whose units I have had the pleasure of studying. With a sharp wit and a particularly keen sense for Australian politics, Dave never fails to provoke thought after reading.

Joel on Software

Master software engineer and co-creator of Stack Overflow, Joel Spolsky has had me hooked since opening my eyes to the simultaneously infuriating and wonderful world of text with The Absolute Minimum Every Software Developer Absolutely, Positively Must Know About Unicode and Character Sets (No Excuses!)

Raw Thought

While I've followed Aaron Swartz's insightful and highly heterogeneous writing since well before 2013, posthumous examination of his prose gains a new dimension in light of the atrocious miscarriage of justice against him.
